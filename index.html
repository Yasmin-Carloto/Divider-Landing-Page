A simple way to do your study

You take a topic and break it down into questions that must be answered. With this, you will be ranked among the same people who are studying the same subject. It is a method inspired by CBL.

Features

Our goal is to make your studying easier by using a simple method that asks you to break down a big topic into a bunch of little questions about it, and then answer those questions.

Learn by yourself

We use a technique inspired by the CBL framework. This characteristic specifies that, when you have a study topic, you, due to your own doubts about the topic, come up with questions about it that must be answered. According to your answer, you will be ranked among other people studying this subject.

FAQ

The purpose is to encourage deeper understanding and engagement with the topic. By formulating questions, learners identify key concepts and areas that need further exploration, enhancing their overall comprehension.

CBL stands for Challenge-Based Learning. It is an educational approach that involves solving real-world challenges through collaborative efforts. This method draws inspiration from CBL by encouraging learners to actively engage with the material and seek solutions through critical thinking and questioning.

This method promotes active learning, critical thinking, and self-assessment. It encourages learners to take ownership of their education and engage more deeply with the material, leading to better retention and understanding.

Educators can introduce this method by guiding students to identify key topics, formulating relevant questions, and facilitating discussions. They can also set up a points and ranking system to motivate students and track their progress.

Questions are typically created by the learners themselves based on their curiosity and areas of interest within the topic. This self-directed approach ensures that the questions are relevant and meaningful to the learner's understanding.
